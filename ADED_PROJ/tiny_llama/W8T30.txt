1. Supervised Learning:

Supervised learning involves training an artificial neural network (ANN) or a deep neural network (DNN) with labeled data. The goal of supervised learning is to train the ANN or DNN to accurately predict the values of the training data.

Reinforcement learning, on the other hand, is more of a methodology, not an approach to learning. Instead of using labeled data, reinforcement learning uses an AI agent to interact with the environment and learn through experience. The agent observes the environment, performs actions, and observes the consequences of those actions, and then learns to make better decisions based on those observations.

2. Unsupervised Learning:

Unsupervised learning involves learning without explicitly specifying the structure of the data. This approach is useful for discovering patterns in data that might not be apparent from labeled data.

Reinforcement learning:

Reinforcement learning involves training an AI agent to perform certain actions based on their successes and failures. The goal of reinforcement learning is to improve the AI agent's ability to perform those actions without being explicitly programmed for them. Reinforcement learning is useful for tasks where the goal is to learn a behavior without explicitly specifying that behavior.

In terms of concrete examples, let's consider the task of detecting objects in an image. Reinforcement learning can also be used for this type of task, as the robot would learn to improve its performance based on its successes and failures.
